## SECTION 1: SHARP MONEY / PROFESSIONAL BETTOR METHODOLOGY

### The Syndicate Model

Professional sports bettors operate as quasi-quantitative hedge funds. Their edge comes from three sources: (1) superior models that produce more accurate probabilities than the market, (2) information advantages (injury intel, insider access to conditions), and (3) operational scale — finding and hammering enough mispriced lines to make small edges meaningful.

### Billy Walters / The Computer Group

**Core Methodology:**
- Built computer algorithms in the late 1980s that analyzed dozens of variables per game
- Targeted lines where their model disagreed with the book's line by a statistically meaningful margin
- Focused heavily on **college football and college basketball** where books invested less analytical resources — the "market inefficiency" was greatest in lesser-watched games
- Achieved a documented **57-60% win rate** on sports bets
- Win rate of 56-60% with significant volume at scale is what separates professionals from amateurs; a 55% win rate betting $10,000/game = ~$5,000/game expected profit

**Factors Their Algorithm Weighted:**
- Strength of each team's lineup (player-by-player value modeling)
- Home/away advantage (with distance traveled as a sub-variable)
- Weather forecasts
- Injury/illness status (real-time intelligence was a competitive moat)
- Each team's previous schedule — bye weeks, consecutive road games, travel difficulty
- Stadium quirks and turf types
- Officiating crew tendencies
- Historical spread performance in comparable situations

**Operational Security / Bet Placement:**
- Used a network of "beards" (proxies / anonymous bet-runners) to place wagers so books couldn't identify the source as Walters
- Spread action across dozens of bookmakers to maximize volume before lines moved
- Once banned from major Vegas sportsbooks, moved to offshore and used runners for domestic placement
- The model: if the team's true line is -6 but the market says -3, hammer -3 at every book simultaneously before the line corrects

**Key Insight:** Walters' edge was largely in markets that were THIN — college sports, second-tier NFL games, games with low public interest. The NFL primetime market is so efficient it's near-impossible to find the same edges. Target thin markets.

### Zeljko Ranogajec / The Bankroll Syndicate

- Australian-based operation employing **300+ people** (spotters, analysts, bettors)
- Placed an estimated **$3+ billion per year** in bets across horse racing and sports
- Operated primarily in pari-mutuel markets (horse racing) where pool size allowed enormous volume
- Key strategy: negotiate **rebate agreements** with bookmakers (TabCorp returned ~8-10% cashback on volume)
- At 8-10% rebate + 2-3% edge = the margin stacks
- Syndicate bet events with **maximum liquidity** — never tried to bet obscure markets where their volume would move the line against themselves
- Model: find small, consistent edges (1-3%) and apply them at massive scale with rebates to compound the advantage

### MIT Blackjack Team Principles Applied to Sports

- The MIT team's core insight was **card counting = exploiting a quantifiable, temporary information advantage**
- In sports betting, the equivalent is: **line inefficiencies exist in the first 24-48 hours after opening**, before sharp money corrects them
- They used team play — multiple agents placing bets at different locations simultaneously to maximize volume before detection
- Applied to sports: syndicate members simultaneously bet the same side at 10-20 books to get full exposure before lines move
- **Big player / small player** structure: small players placed scouts bets to test tolerance, big players came in when limits increased

### The Professional's Operating Rules

1. **Never bet more than 2-5% of bankroll on a single event** (pros use fractional Kelly)
2. **Track every bet, every line, every outcome** — you cannot improve what you don't measure
3. **CLV (Closing Line Value) matters more than win/loss** — if you consistently beat the closing line, you will be profitable long-term regardless of short-term variance
4. **Volume kills variance** — a 500+ bet sample is needed to confirm genuine edge vs. luck
5. **Know your edge, size accordingly** — a 2% edge at Kelly says bet 4% of bankroll, a 5% edge says bet 10%
6. **Market selection is everything** — professionals only bet into markets where their model says they have a calculable edge

## SECTION 2: LINE SHOPPING & MARKET INEFFICIENCY

### The Value of Line Shopping

Line shopping — finding the best available price across multiple sportsbooks — is the single most actionable edge available to a recreational bettor. Conservative estimates place the long-run value of consistent line shopping at **1-3% additional ROI** over betting at a single book.

**Specific spreads:** A half-point difference on a spread matters enormously when that half-point is on or near a key number (3, 6, 7, 10, 14 in NFL). Getting +3 vs +2.5 at the same juice adds approximately 2-3% expected value on that bet alone.

### Markets with the Most Price Dispersion

1. **NFL primetime games (Sunday Night, Monday Night, Thursday Night):** Books know these attract maximum public action and shade lines toward public perception. The "vig-adjusted" price difference between a sharp book (Pinnacle, Circa) and a recreational book (DraftKings, FanDuel) can be 1.5-3 points on totals.

2. **Early-week NFL lines (Sunday night through Tuesday):** When NFL lines open, limits are low ($500-$2,000 at most books) and the line is "softer." Sharp money hits in the first 6-12 hours. If you're a recreational bettor with a model edge, **early week is your window**.

3. **College football and college basketball openers:** Books invest fewer analytical resources in setting NCAAB and NCAAF lines. Opening lines in low-profile games (mid-major conferences, non-ranked teams) are demonstrably softer — this is where the most consistent CLV can be obtained.

4. **MLB daily lines:** Books open lines 24-48 hours before first pitch. Confirmed starting pitcher news, weather information, and bullpen usage data all move lines. The gap between an early book price and the closing price can be 10-20 cents on a moneyline.

5. **Player props:** The most inefficient market in sports betting. Books post hundreds of props per game with limited modeling depth. A bettor with access to Statcast data, pace-of-play data, and defensive matchup data has a structural edge over a book that uses market consensus plus their own thin model.

### Key Situational Inefficiencies

**Primetime TV Games:**
- Public teams (Cowboys, Patriots, Lakers, Yankees, Packers) are systematically overvalued in primetime matchups
- Books shade lines 0.5-2 points toward the public side to balance action
- Fading public teams in primetime has historically produced +1.5 to +2.0 points of CLV on average

**Divisional Games:**
- NFL divisional games are consistently priced differently than non-divisional games
- The underdog in a divisional matchup outperforms its spread expectation — familiarity between teams compresses talent gaps
- In Week 1 divisional matchups: underdogs covered 71% ATS (record: 37-15-1 ATS since 2014)

**Early-Season Public Overvaluation:**
- Teams with strong public perception (Super Bowl contenders, star QBs returning) are overbet by 1-3 points in early-season games before results provide calibration data
- Week 1 underdogs: 53% ATS since 2000 (decade-long sample)

**Line Shopping Tools:**
- OddsJam, OddsPortal, OddsChecker — real-time comparison across 20-40 books
- Target books: Pinnacle (sharpest), Circa Sports, BookMaker, BetOnline for early lines; DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM for recreational closing prices (often stale vs. sharp books)

## SECTION 3: REVERSE LINE MOVEMENT (RLM) — COMPLETE FRAMEWORK

### Definition

Reverse Line Movement occurs when the betting line moves **opposite to the direction of public betting percentage**. This is the clearest publicly available signal that professional (sharp) money disagrees with public consensus and has moved the market.

**Classic Example:** 75% of bets are on the Lakers -5 (public loves the Lakers). Instead of moving to -5.5 or -6 (which would happen if the book was balancing toward the Lakers), the line drops to -4.5. That reverse move tells you sharp bettors are loading up on the Magic +5, and the book respects that action.

### Threshold Rules (Actionable Thresholds)

| Public Bet % on Team A | Line Moves For/Against Team A | Signal Strength |
|------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| 55-65% on Team A | Line moves toward Team B | Weak RLM — monitor |
| 65-75% on Team A | Line moves toward Team B | Moderate RLM — elevated signal |
| 75%+ on Team A | Line moves 0.5+ toward Team B | Strong RLM — significant sharp signal |
| 80%+ on Team A | Line moves 1.0+ toward Team B | Very Strong RLM — high-confidence sharp signal |
| 85%+ on Team A | Any movement toward Team B | Maximum RLM signal |

**Money Percentage Divergence:**
A more powerful signal than ticket count alone: if Team A is getting 70% of BETS but only 40% of MONEY wagered, that means the 30% on Team B are making very large wagers. This "bet-money gap" of 20%+ strongly indicates sharp action.

Rule: **When bets% and money% diverge by 15-20% or more, sharp money is involved.**

### RLM Confirmation Stack (Layered Signal Approach)

For maximum reliability, require 2-3 of these to align before acting on RLM:

1. Public ticket % on one side is 70%+
2. The line moves against that public side by at least 0.5 points (or 10-15 cents on a moneyline)
3. Money percentage is below bet percentage for the public side (sharp bets are larger)
4. The move occurs at a sharp market-making book first (Pinnacle, Circa, Bookmaker)
5. The move happens within a 24-hour window (concentrated, not gradual)

### Best Sports and Markets for RLM

**NFL (Best):** Highest public betting volume means the bet%/money% gap is most visible. RLM is most reliable on Sunday afternoon games with marquee matchups drawing 70%+ public consensus.

**NBA:** Effective but lines move more frequently throughout the week. Look for RLM on nationally televised games where public bet percentages are visible.

**MLB:** Less useful — public percentages are lower by volume, and the market is more efficient at correcting early. Still valid on totals bets where a "blowout game" narrative pushes overs.

**NCAAB:** Very effective in tournament play (March Madness) where casual bettors overwhelm the market. RLM on 15-seeds vs. 2-seeds attracts massive public money on the 2-seed, creating clear RLM opportunities.

### Critical Caveats

- RLM is a **signal**, not a strategy. It fails in roughly 40-45% of cases even when the signal is strong.
- A line can move against public bets for mechanical reasons (vig balancing, arbitrage, limits being adjusted) that have nothing to do with sharp action.
- **False steam** is intentionally created by large syndicates to move lines before hammering the opposite side. The buyback move (see Section 4) is the "real" play.
- RLM is most reliable when the signal appears at **Pinnacle or Circa first**, then propagates to recreational books.

## SECTION 4: STEAM MOVES — IDENTIFICATION & EXPLOITATION

### Definition

A steam move is a **rapid, coordinated, near-simultaneous line movement across 5+ sportsbooks** driven by sharp bettor or syndicate action. The defining characteristics are:

1. **Speed:** Movement happens within 90 seconds to 5 minutes across multiple books simultaneously
2. **Uniformity:** All books move in the same direction, not one or two outliers
3. **Magnitude:** The spread moves at least 0.5 points (or 15+ cents on a moneyline) quickly
4. **Market-wide:** At least 5-6 major books are involved

**Key Differentiator from Gradual Movement:**
"A move from -2.5 to -4 over several hours is ordinary market drift. A move from -2.5 to -4 within ninety seconds across multiple books is not." — That's steam.

### How Steam Is Created

1. A syndicate or large sharp bettor's model identifies a significant mispricing (line should be -7, market says -4)
2. The syndicate coordinates — multiple members or bots simultaneously place large wagers ($25K-$100K per book) at 10-20 books at the exact same moment
3. Each book, seeing heavy one-sided action, adjusts their line
4. Books monitor each other's lines; when one sharp book moves, others copy immediately to avoid being left with stale prices
5. The result: a cascade of movement in seconds across the entire market

### The Head Fake / False Steam Technique (Advanced)

Sophisticated syndicates use a two-step manipulation:
- **Step 1:** Bet heavily on Side A to push the line from -7 to -8.5 (creating false steam on Side A)
- **Step 2:** Other bettors and following-bots pile onto the -8.5, pushing it further to -9
- **Step 3:** The syndicate's **actual position** is Side B (+9). They now hammer Side B at inflated odds they manufactured themselves.

**How to identify:** If a steam move stops abruptly and reverses direction forcefully within 15-30 minutes, you are likely watching the "buyback" — the syndicate establishing their true position. **The second move is typically the real sharp side.**

### Sharp Books to Monitor First

In order of influence and speed-of-information:
1. **Pinnacle** (global sharp market leader, 2% vig, accepts sharp money)
2. **Circa Sports** (Nevada-based, high limits, sharp book)
3. **BookMaker / BetOnline** (offshore, early lines, sharp-friendly)
4. **Betfair Exchange** (UK/international, allows lay betting, true market price discovery)

When one of these books moves by 0.5+ points within minutes, set alerts and watch whether other books follow within 5 minutes. If they do: steam. If only one moves and stabilizes: likely a large individual bet, not coordinated steam.

### Steam vs. Injury News Movement

Critical distinction:
- **Steam:** Line moves with NO corresponding news event (no injury reported, no weather change, no lineup change). Movement is entirely model/sharp driven.
- **News-triggered move:** A starting pitcher is scratched, a QB injury is reported, and the line moves. This is not steam — it's rational market repricing.

In practice: always check injury wire (Rotowire, ESPN injury report, official team injury designations) when you see a significant line move. If no news exists to explain the move, the probability it's sharp action rises dramatically.

### Documented Steam Performance

Sports Insights' steam alert service has historically tracked steam moves. Their data shows mixed results for blindly following steam — because false steam and head fakes pollute the signal. The consensus methodology:
- Use steam as a **confirmation layer**, not a standalone trigger
- Steam at a **sharp book** combined with RLM = high-confidence signal
- Steam at a **recreational book** only = much weaker signal
- **Never bet immediately into steam** — wait for line to stabilize (10-15 minutes), then assess whether it's real or a head fake

## SECTION 5: SITUATIONAL BETTING ANGLES BY SPORT

### NFL Situational Angles

**1. Look-Ahead Spots**
Definition: Team A is playing a meaningless opponent this week but faces a major rivalry game or playoff-seeding game the following week.
Signal: Coaching staff, player interviews, preparation intensity all point toward next week.
Effect: Undervalued teams in look-ahead spots cover at higher than expected rates; their spread price doesn't fully reflect the focus deficit.
Rule: Flag any team where their next opponent is a division rival, playoff contender, or Super Bowl rematch — and the current game is vs. a weak opponent.

**2. Letdown Spots**
Definition: Team A just won a major game (playoff win, rivalry win, nationally televised upset) and now faces a lower-profile regular-season opponent.
Effect: Emotional and physical hangover causes underperformance. The spread price doesn't fully discount for this.
Data: Teams in a letdown spot on a road game after an emotional home win cover at below-average rates.
Rule: Flag any team coming off a nationally televised game (SNF/MNF/TNF win, rivalry game, playoff upset) playing on the road vs. a non-primetime opponent.

**3. Divisional Dog Edge**
- Week 1 divisional underdogs: 37-15-1 ATS since 2014 (71% cover rate)
- Full season: Divisional underdogs outperform expectation because familiarity closes the talent gap that the spread doesn't fully account for
- The market overweights recent performance (which drives public money) and underweights structural advantages of teams that know each other extremely well

**4. Primetime Public Team Fade**
- Cowboys, Packers, Steelers in primetime: consistently inflated by 1-2 points vs. true line
- Public teams in Monday Night/Sunday Night/Thursday Night games receive 65-80% of public bets
- When a public team is getting 70%+ of bets AND the line is not moving in their favor (or is moving against them): clear fade signal
- Historical ROI of fading 60%+ public teams: ~55-57% ATS over large sample

**5. Road Dog Off Bye Week**
- Teams getting extra rest face opponents that have already played that week
- Pre-2011 CBA: bye week edge was 2.2 points per game
- Post-2011 CBA: edge compressed to 0.3 points per game (the market has learned this)
- Residual value: Road underdogs coming off a bye week, facing a team on a short week (Thursday game following a Sunday game). Stack the rest disadvantage.

**6. Team Off Straight-Up Losses vs. ATS Record**
- Teams that lose SU but cover ATS are being undervalued by the public (who judges by wins/losses)
- Track teams with 2-5 SU record but 5-2 ATS record — the market is systematically undervaluing them
- These teams become "buy-low" opportunities before the public narrative adjusts

**7. Super Bowl Runner-Up in Week 1**
- Teams that lost the Super Bowl: 6-15 ATS in Week 1 over 25 seasons (28.6% cover rate)
- Clear emotional/motivational hangover effect
- The market slightly underweights this because public narratives focus on "chip on their shoulder" storylines

**8. Underdog Dominance in Early Season**
- All underdogs: 53% ATS in Week 1 since 2000
- The market underweights uncertainty in Week 1 because it cannot price in preseason preparation accurately

**9. Thursday Night Football Specific**
- Short-rest home teams face road teams who had a full week of prep
- Short-rest teams: decline in offensive and special teams performance
- Short-rest road teams are in the worst possible situation: travel + fatigue
- Fade short-rest road favorites in TNF

**10. Totals: Weather Compressed Games**
- When multiple extreme weather factors align (wind 20mph+ AND temperature below 30F AND precipitation), games go under at dramatically higher rates
- Documented: 6-10 point reduction in combined scoring under multiple extreme conditions
- Books are slow to fully price weather into totals, especially early in the week when lines are set before game-day forecasts are finalized

### NBA Situational Angles

**1. Back-to-Back (B2B) Travel Effects**
Research data (3,005 B2B games, 2013-2020 NBA seasons):
- **Away-Away B2B sequence:** 39.2% win rate (teams playing away game 1, then away game 2)
- **Home-Away B2B sequence:** 36.8% win rate (team plays home then goes on road immediately)
- **Away-Home B2B sequence:** 54.4% win rate (team plays road then returns home)

**Critical insight:** Every additional 500km of travel in an Away-Home B2B sequence **reduces win probability by ~4%**.

**Travel Direction Effect:**
- Teams traveling **eastward** in B2B: 44.51% win rate
- Teams traveling **westward** in B2B: 40.83% win rate
- (Eastward travel is easier because it aligns better with biological circadian rhythms)

**Action rule:** Bet AGAINST teams in Away-Away B2B sequences, especially when they've traveled 1,500km+ from game 1 to game 2. Bet FOR teams returning home (Away-Home) on second night when travel distance was under 1,000km.

**2. 4th Game in 5 Nights (Historical)**
- ATS record: 588-539 (52.2%) overall for opponent of fatigued team
- **After Game 21 of the season:** 451-375 (54.6%) — approaching "blindly profitable"
- NBA eliminated most 4-in-5 schedules starting 2017-18 season, so this angle is rarer
- When it occurs: heavily weight against the fatigued team

**3. Rest Advantage — Quantified**
- When one team has a rest day and the other doesn't: 51.2% win rate for rested team
- Doesn't sound large, but applied over a season and combined with other factors, it compounds

**4. Load Management Detection**
- Before official announcement: monitor social media (beat reporters, team insiders), injury designations ("rest" tag), pregame workout reports
- When a star player is out "for load management," the market adjusts — but if you have the information 2-4 hours before the book does, that's a trading window
- NBA injury report deadline is 30 minutes before tipoff; sharp bettors monitor the official reports the moment they drop
- Tool: Rotowire NBA injury tracker, ESPN injury wire, Twitter/X beat reporters for each team

**5. Pace Mismatch Totals**
- KenPom-equivalent for NBA: use pace ratings (possessions per 48 minutes)
- When a fast-paced team (top 5 in possessions/game) plays a slow team (bottom 5): the fast team usually wins the pace battle, pushing totals up
- When two slow teams meet: bet the under
- Pace mismatch combined with B2B fatigue is a particularly powerful combo for unders

**6. Net Rating as True Measure**
- A team with a top-10 net rating but a losing record (e.g., playing lots of close losses) is systematically undervalued by a public that looks at win/loss
- Net ratings swing >+5 vs. season average correlated with 60% ATS cover in sampled data
- Model: if a team's net rating implies they should be 3 points better than their market line, that's a betting edge

- Why: starter quality is known and analyzable; closing pitcher quality is random; F5 results regress more predictably to underlying starter metrics

- Hard hit rate and exit velocity are the leading indicators for future offensive production
- A team with top-5 xwOBA but bottom-5 actual wOBA is being unlucky — regression incoming

### NHL Situational Angles

**1. Goalie Metrics — GSAA**
- GSAA (Goals Saved Above Average): the most predictive goalie metric for betting
- A goalie with GSAA of +15 has saved 15 more goals than an average goalie would facing the same shot quality
- Elite goalies (GSAA +10 or above): consistently undervalued because oddsmakers rely on team metrics more than goalie-specific modeling
- **Rule:** When a team's starting goalie has GSAA > 10 and the spread/puck line doesn't reflect it, bet the undervalued team

**2. xG and Corsi — Tandem Use**
- xG (Expected Goals): weights shots by quality (location, angle, screen, shot type)
- Corsi% (CF%): all shot attempts (shots on goal + misses + blocks) as a proxy for territorial dominance
- Neither alone is sufficient:
  - High Corsi + Low xG = shooting lots from bad areas (perimeter shots, weak chances)
  - High xG + Low Corsi = fewer shots but high danger (power play proficiency, odd-man rushes)
- **Use together:** A team with both top-10 xGF% AND top-10 CF% is a legitimate powerhouse. A team with high Corsi but low xG is a "phantom dominant" team — they look good in possession stats but aren't generating real threats.

**3. Schedule Clusters**
- NHL teams play 3 games in 4 nights multiple times per season
- Third game of a 3-in-4: significant fatigue factor, especially for goal depth
- Under in 3rd game of clusters hits at elevated rate — goalies are less sharp, defensive intensity drops

**4. Revenge Games**
- NHL has strong anecdotal data on revenge game motivation (team that was blown out by 4+ goals last matchup)
- Quantified impact: disputed, but teams motivated to "respond" to a blowout loss show elevated compete level
- Best signal when combined with: team's home game, recent positive momentum, and opponent's goalie being average or below

**5. PDO (Shooting% + Save%)**
- PDO = team shooting percentage + team save percentage; league average is 1.000
- Teams with PDO > 1.030: running above sustainable luck, likely to regress (fade them as favorites)
- Teams with PDO < 0.970: running below luck, likely to improve (buy them as underdogs)
- PDO corrects toward 1.000 over a season; in-season PDO deviations create betting opportunities

### NCAAB Situational Angles

**1. KenPom Tempo/Efficiency Metrics**
- Adjusted Offensive Efficiency (AdjO): points scored per 100 possessions, adjusted for opponent difficulty
- Adjusted Defensive Efficiency (AdjD): points allowed per 100 possessions, adjusted for opponent
- **Rule:** When the spread implies a certain margin but the KenPom efficiency gap implies a different margin, bet the KenPom side
- KenPom is most valuable for **totals** — when two high-tempo teams meet, pace pushes the total up; two low-tempo teams meeting pushes it down

**2. Tempo Mismatch for Totals**
- If Team A averages 75 possessions/game and Team B averages 65 possessions/game, the actual game pace will be somewhere in the middle (roughly 69-71 possessions)
- Calculate: projected possessions × combined points per possession = expected total
- Compare to the listed total — when KenPom-derived total differs by 3+ points, it's a significant betting signal

**3. Big Conference Road Underdog**
- High-major conference teams (Big Ten, Big 12, SEC, ACC, Big East) in road games at mid-major opponents are consistently undervalued
- These teams have better athletes and systems but the road environment is unpredictable
- The public undervalues big conference teams in neutral road settings vs. top mid-majors — this creates value

**4. Travel and Schedule Factors**
- NCAAB teams crossing multiple time zones for road games (especially East Coast teams to Mountain/Pacific, and vice versa) underperform their spread
- Short rest (2 days vs. opponent's 4 days) correlates with meaningful performance decline, particularly affecting defensive intensity
- The KenPom schedule adjustment metric directly accounts for this — use it

**5. Home Court Advantage — Specific Values**
- Average home court advantage in college basketball: 3.1 points
- Top hostile environments (Duke's Cameron Indoor, Kansas's Allen Fieldhouse, Kentucky's Rupp Arena): home court advantage worth 4-5 points
- Books generally price home court at 3.0-3.5 regardless of venue hostility — this is an exploitable inefficiency for games at elite home environments

## SECTION 6: CLOSING LINE VALUE (CLV) — THE GOLD STANDARD METRIC

### Why CLV is the Ultimate Betting Performance Metric

The closing line at a sharp book (Pinnacle, Circa) represents the market's most accurate assessment of the true probability of an outcome. It incorporates all public and sharp money, injury news, weather, and everything knowable at game time. **If you consistently got better prices than the closing line, you consistently had a mathematical edge.**

CLV is superior to win/loss record because:
- A bettor can go 55-45 (55%) with bad CLV (they just got lucky)
- A bettor can go 45-55 (45%) with excellent CLV (they consistently found value that happened not to hit)
- Over 500+ bets, the bettor with consistent positive CLV **will** be profitable; the one with good win% but negative CLV **will** lose money

**The industry standard:** 75.6% CLV beat rate is "very good" for a sharp bettor. Beating the closing line more than 55% of the time over 500+ bets is a meaningful edge.

### How to Calculate CLV

**For Point Spreads:**
CLV (points) = Your spread bet - Closing spread
Example: You bet Team A -3.5. Line closes at -5. Your CLV = -3.5 - (-5) = +1.5 points (positive CLV = you found value)
Example: You bet Team A -3.5. Line closes at -2.5. Your CLV = -3.5 - (-2.5) = -1.0 points (negative CLV = you bet the wrong way)

**For Moneylines:**
CLV (%) = (Your odds / Closing odds - 1) × 100
Example: You bet Team A at -150. Line closes at -175. CLV = (-150/-175 - 1) × 100 = +14.3% (you got a better price)

**For Totals:**
Same as spreads — track the direction and magnitude of movement from your bet to close.

### CLV Integration into Confidence Scoring

For an AI betting engine, CLV should be used as a **feedback mechanism and weight adjuster**, not just a static metric:

- **Forward-looking CLV estimate:** Before the game, project what the closing line will be based on current sharp action, expected news, and historical line movement patterns. If your projected pick will generate +1.5 CLV points, weight the confidence higher.
- **Historical CLV tracking by signal type:** Track which signal combinations (RLM + steam + situational) generate the most consistent positive CLV. Those signal stacks deserve higher confidence weights.
- **CLV calibration:** After every game, record actual CLV. Over time, this tells you which of your model's predictions are actually identifying market inefficiencies vs. noise.

### Practical CLV Rules for the Engine

1. **If projected CLV is negative (the line has already moved against you), do not bet** — you've missed the window
2. **Ideal CLV target:** +0.5 to +2.0 points on NFL spreads, +5% to +15% on moneylines
3. **A 2-5% edge over Pinnacle closing lines implies 15-25% annual ROI improvement** vs. the average bettor
4. **Bet early on markets where you have information edges** (early week NFL, college sports) to maximize CLV capture
5. **Bet late on markets where news edges are important** (MLB pitcher confirmation, NBA injury report)

## SECTION 7: ADVANCED METRICS BY SPORT

### NFL Advanced Metrics

**EPA (Expected Points Added Per Play)**
- Measures how many expected points each play adds or subtracts
- **Threshold:** Teams with EPA/play of +0.1 or above are elite. Teams at -0.1 or below are poor.
- **Predictive power:** EPA/play is more predictive of future point differential than actual points scored (which includes garbage time, special teams anomalies)
- **Betting application:** When a team's EPA/play ranking significantly diverges from their win-loss record or their spread position, bet toward the EPA/play implied line

**DVOA (Defense-Adjusted Value Over Average)**
- Grades every play against the expected performance against that opponent, then normalizes to league average
- **Key uses:**
  - **Offensive DVOA vs. opponent's Defensive DVOA:** The gap between these two numbers tells you how mismatched a game truly is
  - **Red zone DVOA:** Teams that are top-10 in red zone offensive DVOA but middle-of-pack in total DVOA are being undervalued by books that rely on gross numbers
  - **DVOA in close games vs. blowouts:** Separate teams' DVOA in games decided by 7 or fewer points (the "competitive game DVOA") — this is more predictive for betting since garbage time performance inflates raw numbers

**CPOE (Completion Percentage Over Expectation)**
- Strips out the difficulty of throws to evaluate QB accuracy net of target difficulty
- **Threshold:** +5% CPOE or above = elite; -5% or below = below-average
- **Betting edge:** When a QB with positive CPOE is on a team with a lower implied win probability than their actual efficiency warrants, bet the team
- **Inverse:** When a "stat-padded" QB (high completion% but against easy coverages, many checkdowns) is being overvalued by the public, fade the team

**aDOT (Average Depth of Target)**
- High aDOT (14+ yards): aggressive passing offense, correlated with higher variance outcomes
- Low aDOT (under 8 yards): dink-and-dunk system, lower variance, tends to keep games closer
- **Totals application:** High aDOT teams in games with wind 15+ mph should see totals adjusted down — deep balls are the most affected by wind

**PFF Grades**
- Player-by-player grading on a 0-100 scale
- **Offensive line grade vs. opponent's defensive line grade:** The O-Line/D-Line matchup grade gap is highly predictive of rushing performance and QB sack rate
- **Coverage grade vs. WR grade:** Wide receiver corps vs. secondary grade matchup predicts air yards efficiency

---

### NBA Advanced Metrics

**Net Rating (NRTG)**
- Formula: ORTG - DRTG (points scored per 100 possessions minus points allowed per 100 possessions)
- **Thresholds:**
  - +8 or above: Championship-tier team (top 3-5 in league)
  - +4 to +8: Contender
  - 0 to +4: Playoff team
  - -2 to 0: Bubble team
  - Below -4: Lottery team
- **Betting edge:** A team with +6 net rating being priced as a +3 spread underdog is almost certainly undervalued. The market may be reacting to their record (which includes injury-affected games) rather than their true quality.

**ORTG / DRTG (Offensive / Defensive Rating)**
- **ORTG Thresholds:** 118+: elite offense; 115-118: very good; 112-115: average; below 110: poor
- **DRTG Thresholds:** Below 110: elite defense; 110-113: very good; 113-116: average; above 116: poor
- **Totals application:** ORTG of both teams combined predicts scoring pace more reliably than traditional per-game stats

**True Shooting Percentage (TS%)**
- Adjusts for 3-pointers and free throws
- **League average:** ~58%; Elite: 62%+; Below average: <55%
- **Betting use:** Team with top-5 TS% but 8th seed due to pace or record issues is being sold short by the market

**Pace**
- Possessions per 48 minutes
- **Fast pace:** 102+ possessions (pushes totals up)
- **Slow pace:** 96 or fewer (pushes totals down)
- **Totals calculation:** (Team A's pace + Team B's pace) / 2 × (both teams' combined points per possession) = expected game total
- When KenPom/NBA equivalent-derived total differs from the listed total by 4+ points: significant bet signal

**eFG% (Effective Field Goal Percentage)**
- Adjusts raw FG% for the fact that 3-pointers are worth 1.5x more than 2-pointers
- Teams in the top 5 in eFG% with sustainable shot profiles will outperform their point totals

---

### MLB Advanced Metrics

**FIP (Fielding Independent Pitching)**
- Measures only what the pitcher controls: strikeouts, walks, hit-by-pitches, home runs
- Scale: same as ERA (3.50 is excellent; 4.50 is league average; 5.00+ is poor)
- **Why it matters:** ERA includes luck (defense, BABIP). FIP strips luck out. A pitcher with 4.80 ERA but 3.60 FIP is being severely penalized by bad luck and should be bet on.

**xFIP (Expected Fielding Independent Pitching)**
- Replaces actual home run rate with expected HR rate (based on fly ball rate and park-adjusted HR/FB rate)
- More predictive than FIP for future ERA
- Use xFIP over FIP when a pitcher has an extreme home run rate (either very high or very low) that doesn't match their fly ball profile

**wRC+ (Weighted Runs Created Plus)**
- Adjusts for park and league; 100 = league average; 120 = 20% above average
- **Betting use:** When a team's lineup wRC+ significantly outpaces the pitcher's opposing wRC+ allowed, lean toward the hitting team regardless of ERA narrative

**BABIP (Batting Average on Balls in Play)**
- League average: .300
- Team below .270: likely experiencing bad luck (positive regression incoming — lean over on future games)
- Team above .340: likely experiencing good luck (negative regression incoming — lean under on future games)
- **Critical:** BABIP is a luck normalization tool. Use it to identify when teams are due for regression, not when they ARE performing well.

**DRC+ (Deserved Runs Created Plus)**
- Baseball Prospectus metric; similar to wRC+ but more aggressive about removing luck
- Teams with DRC+ significantly above their wRC+ are being undervalued because the luck component hasn't been publicly recognized

**Statcast Data**
- **Exit velocity (EV):** Average EV of 90+ mph = elite hitter; 87-89 = average; below 85 = poor contact
- **Hard hit rate:** 40%+ = elite; 35-40% = average; below 32% = poor
- **xwOBA (Expected Weighted On-Base Average):** True production indicator stripped of BABIP luck

---

### NHL Advanced Metrics

**xG (Expected Goals)**
- Weights shots by quality: location, shot type, whether there was a screen, game state
- **Strong team indicator:** Top-10 in xGF% (expected goals for %) across full season is more predictive than actual goals for/against
- **xG differential:** A team with +0.5 xG differential per game but even actual goals differential is getting unlucky in shooting/goaltending; regression incoming toward winning more

**Corsi% / CF% (Corsi For Percentage)**
- All shot attempts at both ends; >52% = dominant possession; <48% = weak possession
- **Limitation:** Corsi ignores shot quality. A team shooting from the perimeter generates high Corsi but low xG.
- **Use case:** Corsi is best used as a zone dominance indicator. High Corsi + High xG = legitimately dominant team.

**GSAA (Goals Saved Above Average)**
- Elite goalie: GSAA > +10
- Average: -5 to +5
- Below average: < -10
- **Betting application:** Books often undervalue elite goalies in moneyline pricing. A team with a GSAA > 12 goalie getting a +140 moneyline price when their xG suggests a -110 true price represents a 15-25% edge.

**PDO**
- Sum of team shooting% and team save%. League average always approaches 1.000 over time.
- **Above 1.030:** Lucky; regression to 1.000 expected
- **Below 0.970:** Unlucky; positive regression expected
- **Betting edge:** Teams with PDO < 0.970 are undervalued underdogs. Teams with PDO > 1.030 are overvalued favorites.

## SECTION 8: KELLY CRITERION & BANKROLL MANAGEMENT

### The Kelly Criterion Formula

Kelly Fraction = (bp - q) / b

Where:
- b = odds received on the bet (expressed as decimal minus 1; a -110 bet = 0.909; a +150 bet = 1.50)
- p = probability of winning (your model's true probability)
- q = probability of losing (1 - p)

**Example:** Your model gives Team A 58% win probability. The market is at 52% (implied by the odds).
- b = 0.909 (at -110 odds)
- p = 0.58
- q = 0.42
- Kelly = (0.909 × 0.58 - 0.42) / 0.909 = (0.527 - 0.42) / 0.909 = 0.107 / 0.909 = **11.8% of bankroll**

### Modified Kelly — What Professionals Actually Use

Full Kelly produces aggressive bankroll swings (40-60% drawdowns are common). Almost universally, professional bettors use **fractional Kelly**:

| Kelly Fraction | Growth Rate | Max Drawdown | Variance |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Full Kelly (1.0x) | Maximum | 40-60% | Very high |
| Half Kelly (0.5x) | 75% of full | ~25-35% | High |
| Quarter Kelly (0.25x) | 60% of full | ~15-20% | Moderate |
| Eighth Kelly (0.125x) | 45% of full | ~8-12% | Low |

**Industry consensus:** Most professional bettors use **Quarter Kelly (25%)** or **Half Kelly (50%)**.

**Reasoning for fractional Kelly:**
- Your probability estimate (p) is uncertain. If your model says 58% but the true probability is 54%, full Kelly overstates the correct bet size by ~2x, producing ruin risk.
- Half Kelly: 75% of the growth of full Kelly with dramatically reduced variance and near-elimination of ruin risk.
- Quarter Kelly: Used by risk-averse professionals or when model confidence is low.

### Practical Bankroll Rules

1. **Never bet more than 5% of bankroll on a single bet** (even if Kelly says 15%)
2. **Cap bet size at 3% for single game, 5% for correlated plays** when model uncertainty is high
3. **Minimum sample for Kelly calibration:** 200+ bets at similar edge levels before trusting the Kelly fraction
4. **Reassess monthly:** As bankroll grows (or shrinks), bet sizes automatically adjust upward (or downward) with Kelly
5. **Never chase losses with over-Kelly bets** — the math will catch up and destroy the bankroll

### Sharpe Ratio for Betting

Professional bettors also track Sharpe ratio: (Average return per bet - 0) / Standard deviation of returns
- Sharpe > 0.5: Good risk-adjusted performance
- Sharpe > 1.0: Excellent
- Sharpe < 0.2: Poor — too much variance relative to returns

### Threshold Interpretation Guide

**Bet % Only (Ticket Count)**
- 55-65% on one side: Slight public lean — minimal signal
- 65-75% on one side: Moderate public lean — monitor for RLM
- 75-85% on one side: Heavy public action — look for RLM
- 85%+ on one side: Extreme public consensus — contrarian value likely; books always shade this side

**Money % vs. Bet % Gap (Most Reliable Signal)**

| Bet % | Money % | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| 70% on Team A | 70% on Team A | Public money, balanced — no edge |
| 70% on Team A | 50% on Team A | 20pt gap — sharp action on Team B |
| 70% on Team A | 40% on Team A | 30pt gap — strong sharp money on Team B |
| 30% on Team B | 60% on Team B | Sharp money heavily on Team B, public on Team A |

**The Rule:** When the money% exceeds bet% by 15-20%+ for the smaller side, sharp money is present. The larger the gap, the stronger the signal.

**Confirmed Sharp Action (Highest Reliability)**
1. One side getting 30-35% of bets but 60-65% of the money
2. The line moves in the direction of that 30-35% side (RLM)
3. The move originated at Pinnacle or Circa
4. Move happened in a single burst (steam) rather than gradually

When all 4 align: this is as close to a "confirmed sharp move" as publicly available data provides.

### Contrarian Public Fade System

Public teams receiving 60%+ of bets have a documented 32% ATS cover rate in NFL (vs. 50% theoretical).
However: not all high-public games are equal. The fade works best when:
- Public team is a familiar "media darling" (Cowboys, Packers, Chiefs, Patriots era, Lakers, Celtics)
- The game is in primetime (inflates public action)
- The public side is the favorite (not an underdog)
- There is no strong "sharp" reason to be on the public side (no obvious line value at that number)

## SECTION 12: INJURY IMPACT MODELING

### NBA — Quantifying Missing Players

**Framework:** Use BPM (Box Plus/Minus) or RAPTOR/EPM metrics to quantify each player's value

**BPM Reference:**
- Elite MVP-level player: BPM of +6 to +9
- All-Star caliber: BPM of +3 to +5
- Solid starter: BPM of +1 to +2
- Average starter: BPM 0 to +1
- Bench player: BPM -2 to 0
- Replacement player: BPM -2 (definition)

**Calculating Injury Impact (Spread Adjustment):**
1. Get injured player's BPM: e.g., LeBron James at BPM +8
2. Get replacement player's BPM: e.g., backup at BPM -1
3. Difference: 8 - (-1) = 9 BPM points
4. BPM measures points per 100 possessions; an NBA game has roughly 96-100 possessions
5. Impact over full game ≈ 9 × (minutes played / 48) × 0.01 × possessions
   Example: A player averaging 35 minutes, BPM +8, replaced by BPM -1 player:
   Adjusted spread impact ≈ (9 BPM) × (35/48) = 6.5 points per 100 possessions impact
   Per game impact ≈ 6.5 × 0.96 ≈ 6.2 points
6. This means the team should be approximately 6 points worse — if the market only adjusts 3-4 points, there's a 2-3 point edge on the opponent.

**Key Adjustment Factors:**
- **Game 1 without the star:** Often the largest underperformance (team hasn't adjusted rotations)
- **Games 2-5 without the star:** Team often adapts; adjustment shrinks
- **Team depth:** A team with high-quality backups loses less from a star injury than a team where the star dominates usage
- **NBA is the sport most affected by individual injury** — only 5 players per side vs. 9-11 for MLB and 22 for NFL

### NFL — Quantifying Missing Players

**QB Injury:**
- Starter vs. backup QB: typically worth 7-14 points on the spread depending on quality gap
- When an MVP-caliber QB is replaced by an average backup: spread adjustment of 10-14 points is appropriate
- When a solid starter is replaced by a serviceable backup: 4-7 points
- Market often underadjusts by 2-4 points, especially on short notice

**Non-QB Skill Players:**
- Elite pass rusher absent: 3-5 points benefit to opposing QB
- Top WR/TE absent: 1-3 points depending on target share
- Starting RB absent: 1-2 points (NFL is less dependent on any individual RB than fan perception)

**Offensive Line:**
- Loss of a Pro Bowl offensive lineman: 1.5-3 points on the spread (affects both rushing and QB protection)
- Often undervalued by the market and public

### MLB — Pitcher Replacement Value

**Injury to Starting Pitcher:**
- Elite starter (FIP 3.00-3.40) replaced by a spot starter (FIP 4.80+): 1.5-2.5 run swing on expected total and moneyline
- Market adjusts, but if the injury is announced on game day (after lines have set), there's often a 30-60 minute window before full adjustment

**Best Tool:** FIP differential (starter's FIP vs. replacement's FIP) × expected innings × park factor = adjusted run expectation

### Information Timing (The Edge Window)

- **NBA:** Injury reports due 30 minutes before tipoff. Sharp bettors monitor team injury reports and beat reporter social media to get 1-4 hours ahead of official announcements.
- **NFL:** Wednesday/Thursday injury report + Friday practice designation gives most intelligence. Lines adjust through the week.
- **MLB:** Starting pitchers confirmed ~2-4 hours before game time. Lines are posted as "TBD" or with tentative starters. When pitcher is confirmed, a 30-60 minute adjustment window exists.
- **NHL:** Goalies often announced in warmups — extreme information advantage for those near the arena or following beat reporters. Markets cannot price in goalie scratches until they're official.

### Betting Timing Strategies

**Strategy 1: Early Model Bettors**
- Build your own line before books open
- When your line significantly diverges from the opening line, bet immediately
- Target: 0.5+ points of spread difference or 5%+ moneyline difference
- This captures CLV through line movement toward your fair value

**Strategy 2: Injury/News Arbitrage**
- Monitor real-time injury news (beat reporters, team injury designations)
- When significant injury news breaks before books have fully adjusted: immediate bet
- This window is 5-30 minutes on major news, 30-90 minutes on minor news

**Strategy 3: Weather Timing**
- MLB: Game-day weather forecasts for afternoon/evening games become accurate by 10-11 AM ET
- NFL: Weather forecasts become accurate by Thursday for Sunday games
- Bet weather-sensitive totals (MLB over/under, NFL over/under) when final weather data conflicts with the market's implied total

**Strategy 4: Sharp Line Following**
- Monitor Pinnacle's line; when it moves 0.5+ points in an NFL game or 10+ cents on an MLB moneyline without news
- Follow the sharp book's direction within 5-10 minutes before recreational books copy the move
- This captures the CLV of "riding the steam" — you're following the sharp money rather than identifying the edge yourself

## SECTION 15: CORRELATED PARLAY THEORY

### When Parlays Have Positive EV

Standard parlays are negative EV because sportsbooks price legs as if they're independent, then take their cut on each leg. However, there are specific correlation structures that create true positive EV in parlay betting:

**Rule 1: Correlated Legs Have Parlay Value**
When two outcomes are positively correlated (if one happens, the other is more likely), parlaying them is +EV IF the book doesn't adjust for the correlation.

**The Classic Correlated Parlay:**
College/NFL: Team covers spread + game goes over
- If a team covers the spread by winning convincingly, they've likely scored a lot of points (positive correlation with the over)
- If the spread is -3.5 and the total is 43.5, this correlation is strong: almost every cover involves the team scoring well

**Extreme Correlated Example:**
- Team is -47.5 in college football with a total of 48.5
- Every possible final score where the favorite covers except one (48-0) also sends the game over
- These two bets are almost entirely correlated — parlaying them is strongly +EV if the book prices them as independent

**Rule 2: Same Game Parlays (SGPs)**
- Most sportsbooks now use pricing models that partially account for within-game correlation
- **Where SGP value remains:** Books are less sophisticated about second-order correlations
  - Example: QB Over passing yards + Team wins + Game Over are all correlated and well-priced
  - Less obvious: Home underdog Win + Under total has structural correlation (close, low-scoring games favor home underdogs)

**Rule 3: Home Underdog SGP Angle**
- Home moneyline (underdog) + Under total: positively correlated
- Close, low-scoring games favor home underdogs (crowd factor, familiarity, fewer mistakes)
- Books often price this SGP without full correlation adjustment
- Strongest in: NFL home underdogs of +3 to +7, NBA home underdogs of +3 to +6

**Rule 4: Cross-Sport / Cross-Game Parlays**
- These are approximately uncorrelated (outcomes of different games are independent)
- Parlaying two +EV single-game bets produces a +EV parlay — but with much higher variance
- The math: two bets each with 55% true probability at -110 odds (1.909 decimal)
  - Single bet: +EV at 55%
  - Parlay of two at -110/-110 juice: 55% × 55% = 30.25% true probability vs. ~26% implied — still +EV
- **Key rule:** Only parlay bets where each individual leg is independently +EV. Never parlay to create EV from nothing.

**Rule 5: The Juice Problem**
- Every parlay leg adds the book's vig on top of the prior legs
- Two bets at -110: 52.38% break-even on each. Parlay: 52.38% × 52.38% = 27.4% needed to break even
- If your true probability is 55%: 55% × 55% = 30.25% > 27.4% = still +EV
- But three legs: 55% × 55% × 55% = 16.6% vs. 14.4% needed — still +EV but smaller margin
- As legs grow, vig impact compounds — keep to 2-3 legs unless you have very high confidence on each

### What Combinations to Avoid

- **Team Win + Over total:** Sportsbooks have sophisticated pricing for this — most value has been arbitraged out
- **QB Over passing yards + Team Win:** Well-modeled by books; minimal edge
- **Any SGP where the book explicitly prices correlation:** FanDuel and DraftKings now model most obvious correlations; the "smart" combinations have been priced in

### Where Value Remains in Parlays

1. **Correlated legs at books that don't adjust for correlation** (some smaller or offshore books)
2. **Home underdog + Under (NFL/NBA):** Underappreciated correlation
3. **Two independent +EV plays from separate sports:** Maintains individual edge, variance is only cost
4. **Moneyline underdog + Covering smaller spread:** In NFL, +3 ML underdog + +7 spread parlay: the overlap is a positive-correlation boost

## MASTER AI ENGINE CONFIGURATION — SIGNAL WEIGHTING FRAMEWORK

### Recommended Signal Stack for Confidence Scoring

For each game, the AI engine should evaluate and score the following signals:

**Tier 1 — Market Signals (Weight: 30-35%)**
- RLM confirmation (public % vs. line direction divergence)
- Steam move detected at sharp book (Pinnacle/Circa)
- Money % vs. Bet % gap > 15-20%
- Opening line movement direction and magnitude

**Tier 2 — Advanced Metrics Signals (Weight: 25-30%)**
- Model-derived true line vs. market line gap
- EPA/play, DVOA, Net Rating, xG differential between teams
- Situational metric matchup (pace vs. pace, TS%, GSAA)
- CLV projection (is the current price above or below projected closing line?)

**Tier 3 — Situational Angles (Weight: 20-25%)**
- Look-ahead/letdown identification
- B2B/travel/rest advantage flags
- Weather impact flag with quantified scoring impact
- Umpire/officiating crew signal (MLB)
- Divisional game context

**Tier 4 — Injury & News Impact (Weight: 15-20%)**
- Missing player value calculation (BPM/RAPM gap)
- Starting pitcher confirmation + FIP differential
- Goalie announcement (NHL)
- Recent lineup availability confidence

### Confidence Score Thresholds

| Score | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| 85-100 | 3-4 tier signals aligned, CLV positive, model agrees | Maximum bet (up to 3-5% bankroll) |
| 70-84 | 2-3 tier signals aligned, moderate CLV | Standard bet (1.5-2.5% bankroll) |
| 55-69 | 1-2 signals aligned, thin edge | Small bet (0.5-1% bankroll) or pass |
| Below 55 | No clear signal stack, uncertain CLV | Pass — no bet |

Every pick should output:
1. **Edge type:** Market signal / Metrics edge / Situational / Injury / Weather / Combined
2. **Signal strength:** Number and quality of aligned signals
3. **CLV projection:** Expected closing line vs. current price
4. **Recommended bet size:** Kelly-adjusted, fractional (suggest 25% Kelly as default)
5. **Confidence score:** 0-100
6. **Primary risk:** What breaks the thesis (if star player returns, if weather changes, etc.)
7. **Optimal timing:** When to place the bet (early for CLV capture, late for news confirmation)
